GeoArk vs AEO Check: Which AI Optimization Tool Do You Actually Need?

One monitors your AI reputation. The other audits your AI readiness. They're not competitors — they solve different problems. Here's an honest breakdown.

What GeoArk Does

GeoArk is an AI brand monitoring platform. It queries 7 major AI models — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, DeepSeek, and Llama — and tracks how they describe your brand over time.

Think of it like social media monitoring, but for AI. Instead of tracking what people say about you on Twitter, GeoArk tracks what AI engines say about you when someone asks.

Key Features

  • Visibility scoring — How often do AI models mention your brand?
  • Entity tracking — What attributes do AI models associate with you?
  • Answer gap analysis — Where are AI models recommending competitors instead?
  • Semantic review extraction — What language do AI models use to describe you? ("highly regarded," "trusted," "leading provider")
  • A/B testing for AI — Test different content strategies and measure which ones shift AI model responses
  • AEO schema automation — Generates schema markup with a reported 2.4x correlation to AI visibility

GeoArk is primarily for brands that are already established enough to be mentioned by AI models and want to track and improve their AI-mediated reputation.

What AEO Check Does

AEO Check is a free technical audit tool. It scans any URL and checks 7 factors that determine whether AI systems can find, read, and reference your content:

  • Structured data — Schema.org markup (JSON-LD) that gives AI context about your content
  • llms.txt — A machine-readable file describing your site for AI agents
  • Robots.txt AI rules — Whether you're blocking or allowing AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot)
  • Content structure — Heading hierarchy, semantic HTML, clear page architecture
  • Meta tag completeness — Title, description, Open Graph data for AI summarization
  • Markdown content negotiation — Whether your site serves clean markdown to AI agents
  • Overall AEO score — A 0-100 grade with specific fix recommendations

AEO Check is for anyone who wants to know: "Is my site technically ready for the AI era?" No account needed. No subscription. Just enter a URL and get your score.

The Real Difference: Audit vs. Monitor

Here's the simplest way to think about it:

🔍 GeoArk = The Thermometer

  • Measures your current AI temperature
  • "AI models mention you 34% of the time"
  • "Competitors get recommended 2.1x more"
  • Tracks trends week over week
  • Best value when checked regularly

🔧 AEO Check = The Health Checkup

  • Diagnoses your AI infrastructure
  • "You're blocking ClaudeBot in robots.txt"
  • "Missing structured data on 3 pages"
  • Gives you a fix list, not a trend line
  • Most value on first scan + after changes

One isn't better than the other. They're different tools for different stages of the same problem.

When GeoArk Makes Sense

GeoArk is valuable when:

  • You already have AI visibility — AI models mention your brand, and you want to track whether that's improving or declining
  • You need competitive intelligence — You want to know how AI models position you vs. competitors
  • You're running content campaigns — You publish content specifically to influence AI model responses and need to measure impact
  • You have marketing budget — At $79-199/month, GeoArk is an ongoing investment that makes sense for teams with dedicated marketing spend

The semantic review extraction feature is particularly interesting — knowing that AI models describe you as "reliable but outdated" vs. "innovative but expensive" is the kind of insight that can reshape your content strategy.

When AEO Check Makes Sense

AEO Check is valuable when:

  • You don't know your AI readiness status — Most sites have never been audited for AI optimization. The first scan is usually eye-opening.
  • You're starting from zero — No structured data, no llms.txt, AI bots possibly blocked. You need a foundation before monitoring makes sense.
  • You want quick wins — AEO Check flags specific fixes. Adding structured data or unblocking AI bots takes minutes and can have immediate impact.
  • You want it free — The core audit costs nothing. No trial period, no credit card, no account.
  • You're a developer or technical SEO — The recommendations are actionable and specific. Not "improve your content" but "add FAQPage schema to your pricing page."

The Honest Verdict

Start with AEO Check. Graduate to GeoArk.

If your site is blocking AI bots, missing structured data, or doesn't have an llms.txt file, monitoring won't help — there's nothing meaningful to monitor yet. Fix the technical foundation first. It's free, it takes 30 minutes, and it ensures that when AI models do look at your site, they can actually read it.

Once your technical AEO is solid, GeoArk becomes powerful. Now you're monitoring real signals — how AI models respond to an optimized site — instead of noise from a site that AI can't properly crawl.

Think of it as: AEO Check makes sure the door is open. GeoArk tells you who's walking through it.

GeoArk's subscription model makes sense — monitoring is inherently ongoing. You want to see trends, not snapshots. AEO Check's one-time model also makes sense — an audit is most valuable the first time, then periodically after major site changes.

Together: a free AEO Check audit + a GeoArk subscription costs $79/month. Compared to AEO/GEO agency retainers ($2K-$15K/month), that's a fraction of the cost for arguably better data.

What Neither Tool Does

For full transparency — here's what these tools won't solve:

  • Neither writes your content. They tell you what's working or broken. You still need good content, clear messaging, and genuine domain expertise.
  • Neither guarantees AI citations. AI model behavior is probabilistic. Even a perfect AEO score doesn't guarantee you'll be mentioned. But bad technical setup guarantees you won't.
  • Neither replaces SEO. Traditional search still drives the majority of web traffic. AI optimization complements SEO — it doesn't replace it.

The Workflow We'd Recommend

  1. Audit first — Run a free AEO Check scan. Fix whatever's flagged. This takes 30 minutes to an hour.
  2. Implement basics — Add structured data, create llms.txt, unblock AI bots in robots.txt, ensure clean heading hierarchy.
  3. Wait 2-4 weeks — Give AI models time to re-crawl and re-index your content.
  4. Start monitoring — Set up GeoArk (or similar) to track how AI models respond to your optimized site.
  5. Iterate — Use monitoring data to guide content changes. Re-audit periodically to catch technical drift.

Steps 1-3 are free. Step 4 is where you decide if ongoing monitoring justifies $79+/month. For most small businesses, the free audit is enough. For brands competing in categories where AI recommendations drive real revenue, monitoring is worth it.
