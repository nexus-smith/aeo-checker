## Article

### We Scanned 20 Tech Giants for AI-Readiness — The Results Might Surprise You

AI-powered search tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude are changing how users discover content. But here's the question most SEO teams aren't asking yet: is your website ready for AI agents?

We built an AEO (Agent Engine Optimization) checker that scores websites across seven dimensions of AI-readiness, out of 110 points. Then we scanned 20 major tech companies. The results tell a fascinating story about who's paying attention to this shift — and who isn't.

#### How We Scored

Our checker evaluates seven signals:

- **llms.txt** — A proposed standard telling AI models what content to prioritize
- **robots.txt AI directives** — Whether sites explicitly allow or block AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot)
- **Structured data (JSON-LD)** — Schema.org markup giving AI parsers machine-readable context
- **Open Graph / meta tags** — Titles, descriptions, and social metadata AI uses for summarization
- **Content structure** — Heading hierarchy, semantic HTML, logical content flow
- **FAQ schema** — FAQPage or HowTo schema for direct answer extraction
- **Accessibility & technical** — Performance, mobile-friendliness, semantic landmarks

Each area is scored and weighted for a maximum of 110.

#### Four Key Takeaways

**1. Developer tools lead, enterprise lags.** Sentry (88), Stripe (82), and Vercel (79) top the rankings. These companies ship fast, embrace open standards, and cater to developers who expect clean technical implementations. Enterprise companies (Oracle 35, Salesforce 45, Adobe 38) scored poorly despite vastly larger engineering teams.

**2. AI companies score surprisingly low.** The irony is sharp: OpenAI scores 23/110 on AI-readiness. The company building the AI agents can't be easily found by them. This suggests AEO is still an afterthought even inside AI organizations.

**3. llms.txt is the biggest differentiator.** Companies with a well-structured llms.txt file scored 25-30 points higher on average. It's the simplest signal to implement and the most impactful — yet fewer than 30% of the scanned sites had one.

**4. Structured data separates Good from Fair.** JSON-LD schema markup was the consistent difference between sites scoring above 70 and those in the 40-60 range. Companies that already invested in rich snippets for Google tend to score well for AI agents too.

#### What This Means for SEO Teams

AEO isn't replacing SEO — it's extending it. The foundational work (structured data, clean HTML, proper meta tags) benefits both traditional search and AI agents. But new signals like llms.txt and AI-specific robots.txt directives represent a divergence point.

The data suggests a clear hierarchy of priorities for SEO teams looking to get ahead:

1. **Audit your robots.txt** — Are you blocking AI crawlers without knowing it?
2. **Create an llms.txt file** — Even a basic one signals agent-friendliness
3. **Review your structured data** — JSON-LD isn't optional anymore
4. **Check your content hierarchy** — AI agents parse headings and semantic HTML

The companies that move first on AEO will have a compounding advantage as AI search adoption grows. The ones that wait will find themselves invisible to an increasingly large share of search traffic.
