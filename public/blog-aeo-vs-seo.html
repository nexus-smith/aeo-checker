AEO vs SEO

SEO gets you ranked. AEO gets you recommended. They solve different problems, use different techniques, and produce different results. Here's exactly how they compare.

The short version

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) makes your site rank higher in Google, Bing, and other search engines. It's been the primary web discovery channel for 25 years.

AEO (Agent Engine Optimization) makes your site discoverable by AI agents — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and the growing wave of autonomous tools that browse the web on behalf of users.

They're not competitors. They're complementary layers of the same visibility strategy. But their techniques, metrics, and timelines are meaningfully different.

Key Insight

In 2026, roughly 35% of product discovery starts with an AI agent rather than a search engine. That number was 8% in early 2025. The trajectory is clear — but traditional search isn't dying. The pie is growing.

Shared techniques (good for both)

  • JSON-LD structured data — helps search engines AND AI agents understand your content
  • Fast page loading — both penalize slow sites
  • Clean HTML hierarchy — H1 → H2 → H3 with descriptive headings
  • Clear content structure — front-loaded information, logical flow
  • Mobile responsiveness — assumed baseline for all modern crawlers

SEO-only techniques (don't affect AEO)

  • Backlink building — AI agents don't use domain authority scores
  • Keyword density optimization — AI agents understand semantics, not keyword counts
  • Meta title/description tags — important for search snippets, ignored by most AI agents
  • Internal link architecture — less relevant when agents parse individual pages

AEO-only techniques (don't affect SEO)

  • llms.txt file — purpose-built for AI agent discovery. No SEO value
  • AI crawler permissions in robots.txt — GPTBot, ClaudeBot directives
  • Agent-readable API endpoints — letting agents take actions, not just read
  • Tool descriptions — for agents that use tools (OpenAI plugins, MCP servers)

When you need which

Prioritize SEO when

Your business depends on search traffic — e-commerce, local services, content sites, B2C SaaS. If most of your customers find you by Googling, SEO is still your primary channel.

Prioritize AEO when

You sell tools, APIs, or services that AI agents might recommend — developer tools, SaaS products, consulting services, niche expertise. If your ideal customer asks ChatGPT "what tool should I use for X?", AEO is critical.

Do both when

You're a growing business that can't afford to miss either channel. The good news: the shared techniques (structured data, content clarity, fast loading) give you leverage on both fronts simultaneously.

The real difference: links vs answers

Here's the fundamental philosophical difference:

SEO is about winning a click. You optimize to appear in a list of 10 links. The user clicks one. Your job is to be that one click.

AEO is about being the answer. The user asks an AI agent a question. The agent synthesizes information and gives a direct answer — often citing your site as a source, or recommending your product by name. There's no "list of 10" to compete against.

This has profound implications:

  • SEO rewards volume — more pages, more keywords, more backlinks
  • AEO rewards clarity — one clear, well-structured page beats ten mediocre ones
  • SEO is competitive — you're fighting 10 other sites for position 1
  • AEO is uncrowded — most sites don't optimize for AI agents at all (yet)
Window of Opportunity

AEO today is like SEO in 2003. The rules are still forming, competition is minimal, and early movers get disproportionate advantages. The window won't stay open. As more businesses catch on, the easy gains will disappear.

A practical starting point

If you're already doing SEO, here's what to add for AEO (in priority order):

  1. Add JSON-LD structured data — Organization schema on your homepage, Product/Service schemas on relevant pages. Takes 30 minutes.
  2. Create llms.txt — a plain text file listing your most important pages with one-line descriptions. Takes 15 minutes.
  3. Update robots.txt — explicitly allow GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot. Takes 5 minutes.

Frequently asked questions

Is AEO replacing SEO? ▼
No. AEO complements SEO. Traditional search still drives 90%+ of web traffic, but AI-driven discovery is growing at 40% year-over-year. Smart teams optimize for both. Many AEO improvements — structured data, content clarity, fast loading — also improve SEO rankings.
Can I do AEO without doing SEO first? ▼
Yes, but it's not recommended. SEO provides the foundation — clean HTML, fast site, good content structure — that AEO builds on. If your site has poor SEO fundamentals, AI agents will also struggle to parse it. Start with SEO basics, then layer AEO on top.
What does AEO cost compared to SEO? ▼
AEO is currently much cheaper than SEO. Most AEO optimizations are one-time setup tasks: creating llms.txt, adding structured data schemas, configuring robots.txt for AI crawlers. There's no ongoing link-building or content volume required. The AEO Checker tool is free.
How do I measure AEO results? ▼
Monitor AI agent referral traffic in your analytics (look for sources like chatgpt.com, perplexity.ai, claude.ai). Track whether AI assistants mention or recommend your product when asked relevant questions. Use an AEO checker tool to measure your technical score over time.
What is the most important AEO factor? ▼
Structured data (JSON-LD) is the single highest-impact AEO factor. It gives AI agents explicit, machine-readable information about your business, products, and content. The second most impactful factor is llms.txt — a newer standard specifically designed for AI agent discovery.
